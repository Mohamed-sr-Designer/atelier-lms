10 Illustrator Tricks Every Logo Designer Should Know

MMohamed Tarek·9 July 2026·7 min read

Logo and identity work is the highest-value corner of graphic design, and it lives entirely in Illustrator craft. These are ten tricks I use on every client mark, the difference between a shape that looks drawn and one that looks engineered.

Build on a grid, correct by eye

1. Start every mark on a grid for consistent proportion. 2. But override the grid with optical correction: circles must be drawn slightly larger than squares to look the same size, and horizontal bars slightly thinner than verticals. 3. Overshoot curved letterforms past the baseline. Math gets you close; the eye makes the final call.

Pathfinder & shape discipline

4. Build complex marks from simple boolean shapes with Pathfinder, never freehand. 5. Keep everything on a shared baseline grid. 6. Use the Shape Builder tool (Shift+M) for fast, controlled merging. 7. Expand strokes before delivery so scaling never breaks the weight.

Delivery that survives the real world

8. Test every logo at 16px, if it dies as a favicon, it isn't finished. 9. Deliver a single-color version first; if it works in one color, it works everywhere. 10. Provide proper lockups and clear-space rules so clients can't destroy it. A logo isn't a drawing, it's a system, and the system is what you're actually paid for.

Quick answers

Adjusting shapes so they look correct rather than being mathematically equal, circles drawn slightly larger than squares, horizontals slightly thinner than verticals, because the eye, not the ruler, judges the final mark.
